Neurochemistry of Endocannabinoids Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

What is the primary role of endocannabinoids in the CNS?

  • Excitatory neurotransmission
  • Retrograde neurotransmission
  • Astrocyte modulation
  • Hormonal control
Endocannabinoids act as retrograde messengers, released from postsynaptic neurons to modulate presynaptic neurotransmitter release, typically reducing excitation.

Question 2

Which are the two main endocannabinoids identified in the brain?

  • Anandamide and 2-AG
  • Dopamine and serotonin
  • Norepinephrine and acetylcholine
  • GABA and glutamate
Anandamide (AEA) and 2-arachidonoylglycerol (2-AG) are the two primary endogenous cannabinoids that bind to cannabinoid receptors.

Question 3

Which receptor is primarily responsible for the psychoactive effects of cannabis?

  • CB1 receptor
  • CB2 receptor
  • NMDA receptor
  • GABA-B receptor
The CB1 receptor, highly expressed in the CNS, mediates the psychoactive effects of THC and modulates synaptic transmission.

Question 4

Where are CB2 receptors predominantly located?

  • Brain cortex
  • Peripheral immune system
  • Cerebellum
  • Spinal cord
CB2 receptors are mainly found in immune cells and tissues, playing a role in immune modulation and inflammation.

Question 5

Endocannabinoids are synthesized from which type of molecules?

  • Amino acids
  • Steroids
  • Membrane phospholipids
  • Peptides
Endocannabinoids are lipid-based molecules synthesized on-demand from membrane phospholipid precursors.

Question 6

Which enzyme degrades anandamide?

  • Monoacylglycerol lipase (MAGL)
  • Fatty acid amide hydrolase (FAAH)
  • Acetylcholinesterase
  • Tyrosine hydroxylase
Anandamide is primarily degraded by FAAH (fatty acid amide hydrolase), terminating its signaling effects.

Question 7

Which physiological function is regulated by endocannabinoids?

  • Motor coordination
  • Pain sensation
  • Appetite
  • All of the above
Endocannabinoids regulate a wide range of physiological functions, including pain sensation, mood, appetite, and motor control.

Question 8

Which brain region is rich in CB1 receptors and contributes to motor control?

  • Hippocampus
  • Basal ganglia
  • Frontal cortex
  • Medulla
The basal ganglia are densely populated with CB1 receptors, and endocannabinoid signaling there influences motor activity.

Question 9

What is a unique feature of endocannabinoid signaling compared to classical neurotransmitters?

  • Stored in vesicles
  • Synthesized on demand
  • Fast synaptic transmission
  • Inhibitory only
Unlike classical neurotransmitters, endocannabinoids are not stored in vesicles but are synthesized on demand in response to neuronal activity.
