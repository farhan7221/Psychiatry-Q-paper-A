Neurophysiology of Abnormalities in Physiological Drives Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

Which syndrome is characterized by hyperphagia, hypersexuality, and placidity?

  • Prader-Willi syndrome
  • Klüver-Bucy syndrome
  • Turner syndrome
  • Cushing's syndrome
Klüver-Bucy syndrome results from bilateral lesions of the medial temporal lobes (including the amygdala), leading to hyperphagia, hypersexuality, docility, and visual agnosia.

Question 2

What brain lesion typically causes Klüver-Bucy syndrome?

  • Prefrontal cortex
  • Hippocampus only
  • Bilateral amygdala damage
  • Basal ganglia
The syndrome arises mainly from damage to the amygdala and surrounding temporal lobe regions, which are critical for emotion, aggression, and feeding behavior.

Question 3

Which genetic disorder is characterized by insatiable appetite and obesity from childhood?

  • Prader-Willi syndrome
  • Angelman syndrome
  • Fragile X syndrome
  • Williams syndrome
Prader-Willi syndrome, due to paternal deletion on chromosome 15, leads to hypotonia, hyperphagia, developmental delays, and morbid obesity.

Question 4

Which hormone is typically elevated in Prader-Willi syndrome contributing to hyperphagia?

  • Leptin
  • Ghrelin
  • Insulin
  • Cortisol
Ghrelin, the hunger-stimulating hormone, is elevated in Prader-Willi syndrome, enhancing appetite and contributing to severe obesity.

Question 5

Damage to which hypothalamic area causes hyperphagia and obesity?

  • Ventromedial hypothalamic nucleus
  • Lateral hypothalamic area
  • Suprachiasmatic nucleus
  • Anterior hypothalamus
The ventromedial hypothalamic nucleus is the 'satiety center,' and lesions here impair satiety signaling, causing hyperphagia and obesity.

Question 6

Lesion of which hypothalamic area leads to anorexia and weight loss?

  • Arcuate nucleus
  • Ventromedial nucleus
  • Lateral hypothalamic area
  • Mammillary body
The lateral hypothalamic area is the 'feeding center'; its damage suppresses appetite, leading to reduced food intake and weight loss.

Question 7

What is polydipsia?

  • Excessive eating
  • Excessive water drinking
  • Excessive sleep
  • Excessive exercise
Polydipsia refers to abnormal thirst and excessive water intake, which can be due to hypothalamic dysfunction or psychiatric conditions (psychogenic polydipsia).

Question 8

Which syndrome may present with compulsive water drinking (psychogenic polydipsia)?

  • Schizophrenia
  • Both A and C
  • Bipolar disorder
  • Chronic depression
Psychogenic polydipsia is commonly seen in patients with schizophrenia and mood disorders, characterized by excessive water intake unrelated to physiological need.

Question 9

Which hypothalamic lesion may cause hypothermia?

  • Anterior hypothalamic lesion
  • Posterior hypothalamic lesion
  • Lateral hypothalamic lesion
  • Arcuate nucleus lesion
The posterior hypothalamus is essential for heat conservation. Lesions here impair thermogenesis, causing hypothermia.

Question 10

What is adipsia?

  • Inability to feel full
  • Lack of thirst sensation
  • Inability to sleep
  • Excessive urination
Adipsia is a rare disorder where the normal sensation of thirst is absent, often due to hypothalamic lesions, leading to risk of dehydration.
