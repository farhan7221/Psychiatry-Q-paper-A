Neurochemistry of Glycine Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

What is the primary role of glycine in the CNS?

  • Excitatory neurotransmitter
  • Inhibitory neurotransmitter
  • Neuromodulator only
  • Hormone
Glycine acts as the main inhibitory neurotransmitter in the spinal cord and brainstem, enhancing chloride influx via glycine receptors.

Question 2

Where is glycine most abundant as an inhibitory neurotransmitter?

  • Cortex
  • Spinal cord and brainstem
  • Cerebellum
  • Hippocampus
Glycine is concentrated in the spinal cord and brainstem, playing a crucial role in motor and sensory signal modulation.

Question 3

Which receptor type does glycine bind to?

  • GABA-A receptor
  • Glycine receptor (GlyR)
  • NMDA receptor only
  • AMPA receptor
Glycine receptors (GlyR) are ligand-gated chloride channels that mediate inhibitory neurotransmission in the CNS.

Question 4

Which co-agonist role does glycine have at the NMDA receptor?

  • Inhibitory
  • No role
  • Co-agonist enhancing glutamate action
  • Blocks glutamate binding
Glycine binds to an allosteric site on the NMDA receptor and is essential for its activation alongside glutamate.

Question 5

What ion channel is associated with glycine receptor activation?

  • Potassium
  • Chloride
  • Calcium
  • Sodium
Activation of glycine receptors opens chloride channels, hyperpolarizing the neuron and inhibiting action potentials.

Question 6

What clinical condition is linked to glycine receptor dysfunction?

  • Myasthenia gravis
  • Huntington’s disease
  • Hyperekplexia (startle disease)
  • Parkinson’s disease
Hyperekplexia is a rare genetic disorder caused by mutations in glycine receptor subunits, leading to exaggerated startle responses.

Question 7

Which enzyme is crucial for glycine synthesis?

  • Serine hydroxymethyltransferase
  • Tyrosine hydroxylase
  • Glutamate decarboxylase
  • DOPA decarboxylase
Glycine is synthesized from serine via serine hydroxymethyltransferase, linking amino acid metabolism to neurotransmitter pools.

Question 8

Which transporter is responsible for glycine reuptake?

  • EAAT
  • GlyT1/GlyT2
  • VGLUT
  • DAT
GlyT1 and GlyT2 are glycine transporters responsible for clearing glycine from the synaptic cleft and regulating its availability.

Question 9

Which substance acts as a glycine receptor antagonist and can cause convulsions?

  • Strychnine
  • Bicuculline
  • Picrotoxin
  • Ketamine
Strychnine is a potent antagonist of glycine receptors and can induce convulsions by blocking inhibitory neurotransmission.

Question 10

What is a secondary role of glycine in metabolism?

  • Protein synthesis and as a cofactor for catecholamines
  • Only neurotransmission
  • Mitochondrial ATP synthesis
  • Regulation of calcium channels
Besides its neurotransmitter role, glycine is essential in protein synthesis, heme synthesis, and as a precursor for creatine and purines.
