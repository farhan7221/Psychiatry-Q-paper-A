Neurochemistry of GABA Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

What is the full name of GABA?

  • Gamma-aminobutyric acid
  • Glutamic acid
  • Gamma-aminomethyl acid
  • Glycine
GABA stands for Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid, the primary inhibitory neurotransmitter in the mammalian central nervous system.

Question 2

Which amino acid is the precursor for GABA synthesis?

  • Glutamate
  • Tyrosine
  • Tryptophan
  • Aspartate
GABA is synthesized from glutamate via the enzyme glutamate decarboxylase (GAD), with pyridoxal phosphate as a cofactor.

Question 3

Which enzyme catalyzes the synthesis of GABA from glutamate?

  • Glutamine synthetase
  • Glutamate decarboxylase (GAD)
  • Monoamine oxidase
  • Acetylcholinesterase
Glutamate decarboxylase (GAD) converts glutamate into GABA, a key step in the GABAergic system.

Question 4

What is the main function of GABA in the CNS?

  • Excitatory neurotransmission
  • Neurogenesis
  • Inhibitory neurotransmission
  • Myelination
GABA is the chief inhibitory neurotransmitter, reducing neuronal excitability and preventing overstimulation.

Question 5

Which receptor type mediates fast synaptic inhibition via GABA?

  • GABA-B
  • GABA-C
  • GABA-A
  • NMDA
GABA-A receptors are ligand-gated chloride channels that mediate fast inhibitory postsynaptic potentials (IPSPs).

Question 6

Which receptor subtype is a metabotropic GABA receptor?

  • GABA-A
  • GABA-B
  • GABA-C
  • Glycine
GABA-B receptors are G-protein-coupled receptors that mediate slower, prolonged inhibitory signals via potassium and calcium channels.

Question 7

Which brain region is rich in GABAergic interneurons?

  • Cerebellar cortex
  • Locus coeruleus
  • Raphe nuclei
  • Amygdala
The cerebellar cortex contains a dense network of GABAergic interneurons, crucial for motor coordination.

Question 8

What is a common clinical use of GABA-A receptor modulators?

  • Antipsychotics
  • Anxiolytics and sedatives
  • Antidepressants
  • Cognitive enhancers
Benzodiazepines, which enhance GABA-A receptor activity, are widely used as anxiolytics, sedatives, and anticonvulsants.

Question 9

Which cofactor is essential for GABA synthesis?

  • Vitamin B1 (thiamine)
  • Vitamin B6 (pyridoxal phosphate)
  • Folic acid
  • Vitamin C
Vitamin B6 (pyridoxal phosphate) is a vital cofactor for glutamate decarboxylase, and deficiency can impair GABA synthesis.

Question 10

Which condition is associated with GABAergic dysfunction?

  • Huntington’s disease
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Myasthenia gravis
  • Parkinson’s disease
Huntington’s disease involves the loss of GABAergic medium spiny neurons in the striatum, contributing to chorea and cognitive decline.
