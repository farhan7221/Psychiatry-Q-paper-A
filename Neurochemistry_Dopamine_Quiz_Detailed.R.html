Neurochemistry of Dopamine Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

What is the immediate precursor of dopamine synthesis?

  • Tyrosine
  • DOPA (L-DOPA)
  • Tryptophan
  • Phenylalanine
Dopamine is synthesized from the amino acid tyrosine, which is first converted to L-DOPA by tyrosine hydroxylase, then decarboxylated to dopamine.

Question 2

Which enzyme catalyzes the rate-limiting step in dopamine synthesis?

  • DOPA decarboxylase
  • Monoamine oxidase
  • Tyrosine hydroxylase
  • Catechol-O-methyltransferase
Tyrosine hydroxylase is the rate-limiting enzyme, converting tyrosine to L-DOPA. This step is tightly regulated and essential for dopamine production.

Question 3

What is the primary method of dopamine inactivation at the synapse?

  • Reuptake into presynaptic neuron
  • Enzymatic degradation only
  • Passive diffusion
  • Astrocytic uptake
Dopamine is primarily inactivated by reuptake into the presynaptic neuron via the dopamine transporter (DAT), which limits its synaptic action.

Question 4

Which enzyme is responsible for dopamine breakdown in the synapse?

  • Monoamine oxidase (MAO)
  • Catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT)
  • Both MAO and COMT
  • Acetylcholinesterase
Both MAO and COMT metabolize dopamine. MAO deaminates dopamine, while COMT methylates it, working together to clear dopamine from the synapse.

Question 5

Which dopamine receptor family is linked to Gs protein and stimulates adenylate cyclase?

  • D1-like receptors (D1, D5)
  • D2-like receptors (D2, D3, D4)
  • All dopamine receptors
  • None of the above
D1-like receptors (D1, D5) are coupled to Gs proteins, activating adenylate cyclase and increasing cAMP levels.

Question 6

Where is the major site of dopamine production in the brain?

  • Locus coeruleus
  • Substantia nigra pars compacta
  • Raphe nuclei
  • Amygdala
The substantia nigra pars compacta is a key site for dopamine synthesis, particularly for the nigrostriatal pathway involved in motor control.

Question 7

Which dopamine pathway is most implicated in schizophrenia?

  • Nigrostriatal
  • Mesolimbic
  • Mesocortical
  • Tuberoinfundibular
The mesolimbic pathway, projecting from the ventral tegmental area to limbic regions, is associated with the positive symptoms of schizophrenia due to dopamine overactivity.

Question 8

What is the role of the tuberoinfundibular dopamine pathway?

  • Regulates mood
  • Inhibits prolactin secretion
  • Controls motor activity
  • Regulates appetite
The tuberoinfundibular pathway projects from the hypothalamus to the pituitary and inhibits prolactin release, maintaining hormonal balance.

Question 9

Which metabolite is a major end-product of dopamine metabolism?

  • 5-HIAA
  • Homovanillic acid (HVA)
  • VMA
  • DOPAC only
Homovanillic acid (HVA) is the principal metabolite of dopamine and is measured in CSF or urine as a marker of dopamine turnover.

Question 10

Which enzyme is inhibited by MAO inhibitors to increase dopamine levels?

  • Tyrosine hydroxylase
  • DOPA decarboxylase
  • Monoamine oxidase
  • COMT
MAO inhibitors block the action of monoamine oxidase, preventing dopamine breakdown and increasing its availability in the synapse.
