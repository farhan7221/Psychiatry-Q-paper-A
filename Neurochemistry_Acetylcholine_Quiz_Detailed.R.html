Neurochemistry of Acetylcholine Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

Which molecule is the precursor for acetylcholine synthesis?

  • Acetyl-CoA and choline
  • Tyrosine
  • Tryptophan
  • Glutamate
Acetylcholine is synthesized from acetyl-CoA and choline by the enzyme choline acetyltransferase in the presynaptic terminal.

Question 2

Which enzyme catalyzes acetylcholine synthesis?

  • Acetylcholinesterase
  • Choline acetyltransferase
  • Choline kinase
  • Monoamine oxidase
Choline acetyltransferase catalyzes the transfer of an acetyl group from acetyl-CoA to choline, producing acetylcholine.

Question 3

Which enzyme breaks down acetylcholine?

  • Acetylcholinesterase
  • COMT
  • MAO-B
  • Tyrosine hydroxylase
Acetylcholinesterase hydrolyzes acetylcholine into acetate and choline, terminating its synaptic action.

Question 4

What is the main function of acetylcholine in the CNS?

  • Regulation of heart rate
  • Motor coordination and memory
  • Visual processing
  • Pain modulation
In the CNS, acetylcholine is critical for learning, memory, attention, and motor control, especially in the basal forebrain and brainstem.

Question 5

Where are the major cholinergic neurons located in the CNS?

  • Raphe nuclei
  • Locus coeruleus
  • Nucleus basalis of Meynert
  • Substantia nigra
The nucleus basalis of Meynert, in the basal forebrain, is a major source of cholinergic projections to the cortex and hippocampus.

Question 6

Loss of cholinergic neurons in which area is linked to Alzheimer’s disease?

  • Locus coeruleus
  • Substantia nigra
  • Nucleus basalis of Meynert
  • Dentate gyrus
In Alzheimer’s disease, degeneration of cholinergic neurons in the nucleus basalis of Meynert correlates with cognitive decline.

Question 7

Which receptor type is a ligand-gated ion channel for acetylcholine?

  • Muscarinic
  • Alpha-adrenergic
  • Nicotinic
  • Beta-adrenergic
Nicotinic receptors are ligand-gated ion channels, mediating fast synaptic transmission in both the CNS and peripheral nervous system.

Question 8

Which receptor subtype mediates slow, G-protein-coupled responses to acetylcholine?

  • Nicotinic
  • Muscarinic
  • Beta-adrenergic
  • Dopaminergic
Muscarinic receptors (M1–M5) are G-protein-coupled and mediate slower modulatory responses to acetylcholine.

Question 9

What is a major clinical use of acetylcholinesterase inhibitors?

  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Alzheimer’s disease
  • Schizophrenia
  • Epilepsy
Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors, such as donepezil, are used to treat Alzheimer’s by boosting acetylcholine levels in the brain.

Question 10

Which peripheral nervous system function is mediated by acetylcholine?

  • Sympathetic postganglionic transmission
  • Skeletal muscle activation
  • Vasoconstriction
  • Beta-adrenergic stimulation
Acetylcholine mediates skeletal muscle contraction via nicotinic receptors at the neuromuscular junction in the somatic nervous system.
