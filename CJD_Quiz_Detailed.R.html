Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease (CJD) Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

What is the most common form of Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease (CJD)?

  • Familial CJD
  • Sporadic CJD
  • Variant CJD
  • Iatrogenic CJD
Sporadic CJD is the most frequent form, accounting for about 85% of all cases. It arises without known risk factors or inheritance patterns.

Question 2

Which microscopic pattern is seen in CJD brain tissue?

  • Lewy body formation
  • Spongiform encephalopathy
  • Tauopathy
  • Neurofibrillary tangles
Spongiform encephalopathy is the hallmark of CJD and is characterized by vacuolization in the neuropil of cortical gray matter.

Question 3

What is the genetic mutation associated with CJD?

  • APP gene on chromosome 21
  • Prion protein gene (PrP) on chromosome 20
  • Tau gene on chromosome 17
  • Synuclein gene on chromosome 4
CJD is linked to mutations in the PRNP gene on chromosome 20, which encodes the normal cellular prion protein PrPᶜ.

Question 4

What is the disease-causing form of prion protein?

  • PrPᶜ
  • PrPᴺ
  • PrPˢᶜ
  • PrPβ
The pathogenic form is PrPˢᶜ, a misfolded, protease-resistant variant of the normal PrPᶜ protein that accumulates and causes neurodegeneration.

Question 5

What MRI sign is characteristic of variant CJD?

  • Butterfly sign
  • Hot cross bun sign
  • Pulvinar sign
  • Hummingbird sign
The pulvinar sign refers to bilateral hyperintensity in the posterior thalamus on MRI and is highly specific for variant CJD.

Question 6

Which EEG finding is typical of classic CJD but not variant CJD?

  • Alpha suppression
  • Triphasic sharp waves
  • Generalized spikes
  • Delta rhythm only
Triphasic sharp wave complexes are commonly seen in EEGs of patients with sporadic CJD, but rarely in variant forms.

Question 7

Which protein may be absent in CSF in variant CJD but present in sporadic CJD?

  • 14-3-3 protein
  • Tau
  • TDP-43
  • Huntingtin
14-3-3 protein is often detected in sporadic CJD but is typically absent in variant forms.

Question 8

Which codon polymorphism in the PrP gene affects susceptibility to CJD?

  • Codon 105
  • Codon 129
  • Codon 200
  • Codon 17
Polymorphisms at codon 129 (methionine or valine) of the PRNP gene influence susceptibility. Homozygous M/M is more common in sporadic and variant CJD.

Question 9

Which tissue carries prion agent in variant CJD but not in classic CJD?

  • Liver
  • Brainstem
  • Tonsils
  • Adrenal glands
Tonsillar tissue can harbor prion protein in variant CJD, making it a potential biopsy site for diagnosis.

Question 10

What is a typical gross finding in brains of CJD patients who survive longer?

  • Hippocampal sclerosis
  • Cerebral atrophy
  • Hydrocephalus
  • Pachygyria
Although brains may appear normal early in the disease, patients surviving for months often show generalized cerebral atrophy.
