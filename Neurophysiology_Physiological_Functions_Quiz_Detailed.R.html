Neurophysiology of Physiological Functions Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

Which hypothalamic nucleus is primarily involved in hunger signaling?

  • Suprachiasmatic nucleus
  • Lateral hypothalamic area
  • Paraventricular nucleus
  • Ventromedial hypothalamic nucleus
The lateral hypothalamic area is known as the 'feeding center.' Its activation stimulates hunger and food-seeking behavior; lesions here cause anorexia.

Question 2

Which hypothalamic nucleus is known as the 'satiety center'?

  • Lateral hypothalamic area
  • Ventromedial hypothalamic nucleus
  • Arcuate nucleus
  • Mammillary body
The ventromedial hypothalamic nucleus inhibits hunger when activated, and lesions in this area cause hyperphagia and obesity.

Question 3

Which brain structure is the master regulator of circadian rhythms?

  • Pineal gland
  • Suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN)
  • Hippocampus
  • Amygdala
The SCN of the hypothalamus receives light input via the retina and orchestrates daily biological rhythms, including sleep-wake cycles.

Question 4

Which nucleus regulates thirst?

  • Arcuate nucleus
  • Organum vasculosum of the lamina terminalis (OVLT)
  • Mammillary body
  • Subthalamic nucleus
The OVLT and surrounding structures detect osmotic pressure changes and signal thirst, coordinating water intake.

Question 5

Which neurotransmitter is most involved in pain modulation in the descending pathway?

  • Serotonin
  • Acetylcholine
  • Histamine
  • GABA
Serotonin, released from the raphe nuclei, plays a key role in descending inhibition of pain by modulating spinal nociceptive neurons.

Question 6

Which hypothalamic nucleus is key for thermoregulation and heat dissipation?

  • Posterior hypothalamus
  • Anterior hypothalamus
  • Mammillary body
  • Dorsomedial nucleus
The anterior hypothalamus controls heat dissipation mechanisms (like sweating). Lesions here can cause hyperthermia.

Question 7

Which hypothalamic area is crucial for initiating shivering and conserving heat?

  • Posterior hypothalamus
  • Preoptic area
  • Paraventricular nucleus
  • Lateral hypothalamus
The posterior hypothalamus activates thermogenesis and conserves body heat. Damage here can lead to hypothermia.

Question 8

Which peptide hormone stimulates appetite and is secreted by the stomach?

  • Leptin
  • Ghrelin
  • Orexin
  • Neuropeptide Y
Ghrelin is produced by the stomach and signals the hypothalamus to stimulate hunger, rising before meals and falling after eating.

Question 9

Which hormone, secreted by adipocytes, signals satiety?

  • Leptin
  • Ghrelin
  • Insulin
  • Cholecystokinin
Leptin is released by adipose tissue and suppresses hunger by acting on hypothalamic receptors to promote satiety and regulate energy balance.

Question 10

Which brainstem nucleus integrates visceral sensory input, including gut distension?

  • Raphe nuclei
  • Red nucleus
  • Nucleus tractus solitarius (NTS)
  • Edinger-Westphal nucleus
The NTS, located in the medulla, receives afferent input from cranial nerves IX and X and plays a vital role in autonomic reflexes like digestion.
