Neurochemistry of Serotonin Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

What amino acid is the precursor of serotonin synthesis?

  • Tyrosine
  • Tryptophan
  • Phenylalanine
  • Histidine
Serotonin (5-HT) is synthesized from tryptophan. Tryptophan hydroxylase converts it to 5-hydroxytryptophan, which is then decarboxylated to form serotonin.

Question 2

Which enzyme catalyzes the rate-limiting step of serotonin synthesis?

  • Aromatic L-amino acid decarboxylase
  • Tyrosine hydroxylase
  • Tryptophan hydroxylase
  • MAO-A
Tryptophan hydroxylase is the rate-limiting enzyme in serotonin biosynthesis, converting tryptophan into 5-hydroxytryptophan.

Question 3

Which enzyme breaks down serotonin?

  • COMT
  • MAO-A
  • DOPA decarboxylase
  • Tyrosinase
Serotonin is degraded by monoamine oxidase A (MAO-A) into 5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid (5-HIAA), which is excreted in urine.

Question 4

What is the main metabolite of serotonin measured in CSF?

  • 5-HIAA
  • VMA
  • Homovanillic acid
  • DOPAC
5-Hydroxyindoleacetic acid (5-HIAA) is the primary metabolite of serotonin and is measured to assess serotonin turnover.

Question 5

Where is the primary site of serotonin production in the brain?

  • Substantia nigra
  • Locus coeruleus
  • Raphe nuclei
  • Caudate nucleus
The raphe nuclei, located in the brainstem, are the principal source of serotonergic neurons that project throughout the CNS.

Question 6

Which serotonin receptor subtype is a ligand-gated ion channel?

  • 5-HT1A
  • 5-HT2A
  • 5-HT3
  • 5-HT4
The 5-HT3 receptor is unique among serotonin receptors as it is a ligand-gated ion channel, mediating fast synaptic transmission.

Question 7

What CNS function is serotonin most strongly linked to?

  • Motor control
  • Mood, sleep, and appetite regulation
  • Vision
  • Voluntary movement
Serotonin plays a key role in mood regulation, sleep cycles, appetite, and pain perception.

Question 8

SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors) primarily act by:

  • Inhibiting serotonin synthesis
  • Blocking serotonin degradation
  • Inhibiting serotonin reuptake
  • Stimulating serotonin release
SSRIs block the serotonin transporter (SERT), preventing reuptake into the presynaptic neuron and increasing synaptic serotonin levels.

Question 9

Which condition is associated with decreased CSF 5-HIAA levels?

  • Schizophrenia
  • Depression and suicide risk
  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Huntington’s disease
Low levels of 5-HIAA in CSF have been linked to depression and suicidal behavior, suggesting serotonin deficiency.

Question 10

Which serotonin receptor is targeted by atypical antipsychotics to reduce side effects?

  • 5-HT1A
  • 5-HT2A
  • 5-HT3
  • 5-HT7
5-HT2A antagonism is a key feature of many atypical antipsychotics, helping to mitigate extrapyramidal side effects and enhance efficacy.
