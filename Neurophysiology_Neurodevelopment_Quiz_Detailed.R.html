Neurophysiology of Neurodevelopment Quiz

Prepared by Dr Khalid Farhan

Total Questions: 10

Question 1

What is neurogenesis?

  • Formation of synapses
  • Generation of new neurons from progenitor cells
  • Death of neurons
  • Axon myelination
Neurogenesis refers to the birth of new neurons, particularly prominent during embryonic development and, to a lesser extent, in adult regions like the hippocampus.

Question 2

During which gestational weeks is peak neurogenesis observed in humans?

  • 2–6 weeks
  • 8–20 weeks
  • 25–35 weeks
  • After birth
Peak neurogenesis occurs between weeks 8 and 20 of gestation, laying down the foundation for the developing brain's architecture.

Question 3

What is radial migration in neurodevelopment?

  • Neurons moving along axons
  • Neurons migrating along radial glial fibers from ventricular zone to cortex
  • Synaptic pruning
  • Myelination of axons
Radial migration is the process by which immature neurons travel outward along radial glial scaffolding to reach their cortical destinations.

Question 4

What is tangential migration?

  • Movement of axons toward targets
  • Neuronal migration parallel to the brain surface
  • Radial movement along glial fibers
  • Glial cell proliferation
Tangential migration allows neurons, especially interneurons, to move parallel to the pial surface, enabling proper cortical layering.

Question 5

What is synaptogenesis?

  • Formation of new neurons
  • Formation of synaptic connections between neurons
  • Death of synapses
  • Disassembly of neural circuits
Synaptogenesis is the process of forming new synapses, critical for building neural networks and refining brain function during development.

Question 6

When does peak synaptogenesis occur in the human brain?

  • Prenatal period
  • First 2 years after birth
  • Teenage years
  • After age 30
Synaptic density rapidly increases in early postnatal life, peaking in the first 2 years, especially in the cortex, facilitating learning and sensory processing.

Question 7

What is synaptic pruning?

  • Formation of myelin
  • Elimination of excess synapses
  • Generation of new neurons
  • Degeneration of axons
Synaptic pruning is a refinement process where excess or weak synapses are removed, optimizing neural circuitry for efficient function.

Question 8

In which brain region does adult neurogenesis predominantly occur?

  • Prefrontal cortex
  • Hippocampus (dentate gyrus)
  • Basal ganglia
  • Cerebellum
Adult neurogenesis is well-documented in the subgranular zone of the dentate gyrus of the hippocampus, linked to learning and memory.

Question 9

Which molecular signal is crucial for neuronal survival and synaptogenesis?

  • GABA
  • Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF)
  • Dopamine
  • Glutamate
BDNF promotes neuronal survival, synaptic formation, and plasticity, playing a central role in neurodevelopment and adaptation.

Question 10

Which disorder is associated with disrupted synaptic pruning in adolescence?

  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Schizophrenia
  • Alzheimer’s disease
  • Huntington’s disease
Schizophrenia has been linked to excessive synaptic pruning during adolescence, potentially contributing to the loss of grey matter and functional deficits.
