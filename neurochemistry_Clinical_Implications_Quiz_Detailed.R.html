Question 1

What is the concept of 'aberrant salience' in psychosis?

  • Misinterpretation of random events
  • Assignment of inappropriate importance to irrelevant stimuli
  • Increased dopamine receptor density
  • NMDA receptor blockade
Kapur’s theory of aberrant salience proposes that excess dopamine activity causes individuals to attribute significance to trivial stimuli, contributing to delusions and hallucinations.

Question 2

Which neurotransmitter system is central to the aberrant salience model of psychosis?

  • Serotonin
  • Dopamine
  • GABA
  • Glutamate
The aberrant salience model emphasizes the hyperdopaminergic state, linking dopamine dysregulation to the emergence of psychotic symptoms.

Question 3

How do antipsychotics mitigate aberrant salience?

  • Decreasing serotonin synthesis
  • Blunting dopamine-driven abnormal salience attribution
  • Enhancing glutamate signaling
  • Increasing GABAergic tone
Antipsychotics dampen the dopamine system, reducing inappropriate tagging of irrelevant stimuli with salience.

  • Dopamine
  • Noradrenaline
  • Acetylcholine

  • Increased density
  • No change
  • Decreased density
  • Upregulated affinity only
Depressed individuals often show reduced 5-HT1A receptor density in cortical and limbic regions, correlating with mood symptoms.

  • No change
  • Downregulation of post-synaptic 5-HT2 receptors

Question 7

Which receptor type is involved in the sympathetic features of anxiety?

  • Muscarinic
  • Dopaminergic
  • Beta-adrenergic
  • Histaminergic
β-adrenergic receptors mediate many of the physical symptoms of anxiety, such as palpitations and tremors.

Question 8

What therapeutic effect do beta-blockers have in anxiety?

  • Reduce psychosis
  • Enhance mood
  • Alleviate peripheral autonomic symptoms
  • Stimulate serotonin production
β-blockers like propranolol blunt peripheral adrenergic responses, reducing symptoms like tachycardia and shaking in anxiety.

Question 9

  • Increased acetylcholine release
  • Marked reduction in brain acetylcholine levels
  • Elevated dopamine
  • Hyperactive cholinergic system
DLB is characterized by a significant loss of cholinergic neurons, contributing to cognitive decline.

Question 10

Which treatment is beneficial for cognitive symptoms in DLB?

  • MAO inhibitors
  • Beta-blockers
  • Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors (e.g., rivastigmine)
  • Dopamine agonists
AChE inhibitors enhance acetylcholine availability and are standard care for improving cognitive and psychiatric symptoms in DLB.

Question 11

What sleep-related issue is common in DLB and responds to AChE inhibitors?

  • Insomnia
  • REM sleep behavior disorder
  • Narcolepsy
  • Sleep apnea
REM sleep disturbances, including vivid dreams and acting out movements, are hallmark features of DLB and may improve with cholinergic treatment.

Question 12

  • Weight loss
  • Severe sensitivity and worsening of motor symptoms
  • Tachycardia
  • Increased sleep
DLB patients are highly sensitive to antipsychotics, which can precipitate severe parkinsonism and worsened neuropsychiatric symptoms.

Question 14

  • Noradrenergic system

  • Dopaminergic system
