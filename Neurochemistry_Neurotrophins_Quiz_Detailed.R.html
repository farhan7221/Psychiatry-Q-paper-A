Question 1

What is the primary function of neurotrophins?

  • Inhibit synaptic transmission
  • Promote neuronal survival and growth
  • Regulate blood pressure
  • Mediate muscle contraction
Neurotrophins are a family of proteins that promote the survival, development, and function of neurons throughout life.

Question 2

Which neurotrophin is most closely linked to long-term potentiation (LTP) and synaptic plasticity?

  • NGF
  • BDNF
  • NT-3
  • NT-4
Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF) plays a crucial role in synaptic plasticity, learning, and memory through its effects on LTP.

Question 3

What receptor family mediates neurotrophin signaling?

  • Ionotropic receptors
  • G-protein coupled receptors
  • Trk receptors
  • Cytokine receptors
Neurotrophins exert their effects via Trk (tropomyosin receptor kinase) receptors, with each neurotrophin binding to a specific Trk subtype.

Question 4

Which receptor does NGF primarily bind to?

  • TrkA
  • TrkB
  • TrkC
  • p75
Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) binds with high affinity to the TrkA receptor, initiating downstream signaling for neuronal survival and growth.

Question 5

Which receptor is common to all neurotrophins and can induce apoptosis under certain conditions?

  • TrkA
  • TrkB
  • TrkC
  • p75 neurotrophin receptor
The p75 neurotrophin receptor (p75^NTR) binds all neurotrophins and can mediate apoptosis or survival depending on context and co-receptors.

Question 6

  • NGF
  • BDNF
  • NT-3
  • NT-4
Neurotrophin-3 (NT-3) is essential for the development and maintenance of proprioceptive sensory neurons.
